# BBB Reseller Safety Tips — Guidance Source

**Source:** Better Business Bureau  
**URL:** https://www.bbb.org/  
**Topic:** Ticket scam patterns on social media and resale platforms

---

## Overview

The BBB documents widespread ticket fraud originating on social media platforms including Facebook Marketplace, Instagram, Twitter/X, and Craigslist. Scammers exploit the urgency of high-demand events and use emotional stories to lower buyer defenses.

---

## Most Common Social Media Ticket Scam Patterns

### 1. Sympathy Story + Urgency
The most common scam narrative involves a personal emergency that forces the seller to sell quickly: divorce, illness, family emergency, work conflict. These stories are designed to create goodwill and rush the buyer past due diligence.

**Example scripts:**
- "My sister just had a baby and I can't go."
- "My boyfriend and I broke up right before the show."
- "I got called into work last minute."

These stories may be real — but they should never be a reason to bypass standard verification.

### 2. Off-Platform Communication Redirect
Scammers move buyers to WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal, or email to avoid platform monitoring and dispute resolution. Once you are off-platform, the marketplace cannot help you recover funds.

**Red flag language:**
- "Text me on WhatsApp"
- "DM me on Instagram"
- "Email me directly"
- "The platform chat is unreliable"

### 3. Payment Before Transfer
The scammer insists on payment before initiating the ticket transfer. On legitimate platforms, transfer happens before or simultaneously with payment — not after.

### 4. Fake Seller Profiles
Scammers create fake profiles with stolen photos and fabricated review histories. On Facebook, they often use recently created accounts or accounts with limited activity.

---

## BBB's Recommended Verification Steps

Before paying for any ticket:

1. **Verify the ticket is in the seller's account.** Ask the seller to show you a screenshot of their Ticketmaster/platform account with the ticket listed under their name.
2. **Initiate the official transfer first.** The ticket should appear in your account before any payment is made.
3. **Check the seller's profile age and activity.** A profile created recently with no other content is a red flag.
4. **Search the BBB Scam Tracker** at bbb.org/scamtracker for known patterns related to the event or platform.
5. **Never pay with irreversible methods.** If a seller insists on Zelle, Venmo, Cash App, wire transfer, or gift cards — walk away.

---

## If You Already Paid and Suspect Fraud

Act immediately:

1. **Contact your bank or payment provider.** Call the number on the back of your card. Ask about dispute or chargeback options. Time is critical — some windows are as short as 24-48 hours.
2. **File a report with the FTC** at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
3. **File a report on the BBB Scam Tracker** at bbb.org/scamtracker. This helps warn other buyers.
4. **Report the seller to the platform** (Facebook, Craigslist, Instagram). Provide screenshots of the conversation.
5. **File a local police report** if the amount is significant. You will need this for insurance or bank dispute purposes.

---

## Key Risk Signals (BBB Guidance)

| Signal | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Seller provides personal emergency story to explain urgency | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| Communication redirected to WhatsApp / Telegram / email | HIGH |
| Seller insists on payment before transfer | HIGH |
| New or low-activity social media profile | MEDIUM |
| No reviews or ratings on resale platform | MEDIUM |
| Irreversible payment method requested | HIGH |
| Buyer already sent money and seller is unresponsive | CONTAINMENT |

---

## Containment Steps (Already Paid)

> "If you have already paid and the tickets have not arrived or the seller has gone silent:
> 1. Contact your bank or payment app immediately to request a dispute.
> 2. File a report at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
> 3. Report the seller to the platform (Facebook, Craigslist, etc.).
> 4. File a BBB Scam Tracker report at bbb.org/scamtracker.
> 5. Consider filing a local police report."
