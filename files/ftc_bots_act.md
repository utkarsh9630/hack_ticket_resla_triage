# FTC BOTS Act — Guidance Source

**Source:** Federal Trade Commission  
**URL:** https://www.ftc.gov/  
**Topic:** Better Online Ticket Sales (BOTS) Act of 2016

---

## What Is the BOTS Act?

The Better Online Ticket Sales (BOTS) Act, signed into law in 2016, makes it illegal to:

1. Use automated software (bots) to purchase event tickets in quantities that exceed the maximum per customer set by the ticket issuer.
2. Resell tickets that were obtained using such bots.
3. Circumvent security measures put in place by official ticket sellers to limit bulk purchasing.

The FTC is authorized to enforce the BOTS Act and can bring action against violators.

---

## Why It Matters for TicketGuard

Tickets obtained via bots are often:
- Resold at inflated prices by third-party sellers
- Sold through informal channels (social media, Craigslist) to avoid detection
- Difficult to verify as legitimate

If a seller is offering a large block of premium tickets, has no verifiable purchase history, or cannot explain the source of the tickets, there is elevated risk that the inventory was obtained illegally.

---

## Consumer Guidance

- Report suspected bot-purchased tickets to the FTC at **reportfraud.ftc.gov**
- If you purchased tickets that turned out to be invalid, you may have remedies under consumer protection law
- Use official resale channels that validate ticket authenticity before transfer

---

## Key Risk Signals (BOTS-Related)

| Signal | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Large block of premium tickets available through informal channel | MEDIUM |
| Seller cannot explain original purchase method | MEDIUM |
| Price significantly above face value with no legitimate markup reason | LOW-MEDIUM |
