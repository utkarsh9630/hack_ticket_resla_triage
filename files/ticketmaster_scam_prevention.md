# Ticketmaster Scam Prevention Tips — Guidance Source

**Source:** Ticketmaster Blog  
**URL:** https://blog.ticketmaster.com/ticketing-scams-tips-best-practices/  
**Topic:** Identifying and avoiding ticket scams

---

## Overview

Ticketmaster identifies common scam patterns targeting ticket buyers and recommends using only official, verified transfer mechanisms. Tickets that pass through Ticketmaster's Fan-to-Fan Transfer system are validated and appear directly in the buyer's Ticketmaster account.

---

## Most Common Scam Patterns

### 1. Screenshot / PDF / Barcode Image Scams
The seller sends a screenshot of a barcode or a PDF of the ticket. These are trivially easy to fake or duplicate. The same barcode can be shared with dozens of buyers — only the first to scan it gains entry. **Never buy a ticket from a photo or PDF.**

### 2. Peer-to-Peer Payment Scams
The seller requests payment via Venmo, Cash App, Zelle, PayPal Friends & Family, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency. These methods have no buyer protection and payments are effectively irreversible. Once money is sent, there is no recourse.

**Safe payment methods for tickets:**
- Ticketmaster platform payment
- Credit card via an accredited resale platform (StubHub, Vivid Seats, SeatGeek)
- PayPal Goods & Services (NOT Friends & Family)

### 3. Broken Transfer Excuse
A common scam script: "I can't use Ticketmaster Transfer because my account is flagged / locked / having issues." This is almost always fabricated. Legitimate sellers have no reason their transfer tool would be broken.

### 4. Price Too Good to Be True
Tickets priced far below market value (especially for high-demand events) are a significant red flag. Scammers use low prices to generate urgency and bypass due diligence.

### 5. In-Person Meetup Scams
Seller insists on meeting in person to exchange a printed ticket. The ticket may be fake, a duplicate, or cancelled before entry.

---

## Ticketmaster's Official Advice

- Only purchase from Ticketmaster.com, the venue box office, or authorized resale partners.
- Use Ticketmaster's official Fan-to-Fan Transfer — the ticket will appear in your account before you pay.
- Check the seller's verification status and review history on any resale platform.
- If you receive a suspicious listing, report it to Ticketmaster support.
- For sold-out events, check Ticketmaster's official resale section — fan-to-fan transfers are available there with full protection.

---

## Key Risk Signals (Ticketmaster Guidance)

| Signal | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Seller sends PDF, screenshot, or barcode image | HIGH |
| Payment requested via Venmo, Cash App, Zelle, gift card, wire | HIGH |
| Seller claims Ticketmaster Transfer is broken / unavailable | HIGH |
| Price significantly below market or face value | MEDIUM |
| Seller insists on in-person exchange | MEDIUM |
| Seller has no review history on platform | MEDIUM |
