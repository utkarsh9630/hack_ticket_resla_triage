## 1. FTC BOTS Act

**Source:** Federal Trade Commission  
**URL:** https://www.ftc.gov/

### Summary

The Better Online Ticket Sales (BOTS) Act of 2016 prohibits the use of automated software (bots) to purchase event tickets in excess of the posted maximum number per customer and to resell those tickets above face value.

### Key Points

- It is illegal to use bots to circumvent ticket purchase limits on authorized ticket sellers.
- Reselling tickets obtained via bots is also prohibited.
- The FTC can take enforcement action against violators.
- Consumers who suspect bot-purchased tickets should report to the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov.

### Risk Signals Addressed

- Listings offering large blocks of premium tickets at inflated prices may involve bot-purchased inventory.
- Sellers who cannot explain where tickets were obtained may be reselling bot-acquired inventory.

## 2. FTC Enforcement — Ticket Reseller Actions

**Source:** Federal Trade Commission  
**URL:** https://www.ftc.gov/

### Summary

The FTC has taken enforcement action against deceptive ticket resellers who misrepresent ticket availability, pricing, and official affiliation. The FTC requires that resellers clearly disclose when they are not the official ticket issuer.

### Key Points

- Resellers must not misrepresent themselves as the official box office or venue.
- Ticket prices must be clearly disclosed, including all fees.
- Fake urgency tactics ("only 2 left") may constitute deceptive practice if false.
- Consumers have the right to dispute unauthorized charges with their credit card issuer.
- Safer payment methods: use a credit card when possible, as it provides chargeback rights.

### Risk Signals Addressed

- Seller claims to be "official" but directs buyer off-platform.
- Excessive, undisclosed fees added at checkout.
- False scarcity language used to rush the buyer.

## 3. Ticketmaster Scam Prevention Tips

**Source:** Ticketmaster Blog  
**URL:** https://blog.ticketmaster.com/ticketing-scams-tips-best-practices/

### Summary

Ticketmaster identifies common ticket scam patterns and advises buyers to use only official transfer mechanisms. Tickets purchased through Ticketmaster's Fan-to-Fan Transfer system are verified and protected.

### Key Points

- Always buy from official sources or resellers affiliated with the venue.
- Use Ticketmaster's official transfer feature — it validates the ticket in the buyer's account.
- Never pay via wire transfer, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or peer-to-peer apps (Venmo, Cash App, Zelle) for ticket transactions — these are irreversible and unprotected.
- Be suspicious of ticket screenshots, PDFs, or email attachments — these can be faked.
- Be suspicious of sellers who refuse to use official ticket transfer and insist on in-person meetup.
- Check the seller's profile age and review history on the platform.
- If a deal looks too good to be true, it usually is.

### Risk Signals Addressed

- Seller sends a PDF, screenshot, or barcode image instead of initiating a platform transfer.
- Seller insists on Venmo, Cash App, Zelle, or wire payment.
- Seller refuses to use Ticketmaster Transfer or equivalent.
- Price is significantly below face value.

## 4. BBB Reseller Safety Tips

**Source:** Better Business Bureau  
**URL:** https://www.bbb.org/

### Summary

The BBB documents widespread ticket fraud on social media and resale platforms, particularly on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and Instagram. Scammers often pose as individuals urgently selling tickets due to life circumstances.

### Key Points

- Most ticket scams on social media involve sellers who cannot use official transfer tools.
- Urgency is a deliberate pressure tactic — legitimate sellers are not typically in a rush.
- Off-platform communication (WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal) is a major red flag.
- Never pay before the ticket appears in your own account via an official transfer.
- Check the BBB Scam Tracker for reported patterns: bbb.org/scamtracker.
- When in doubt, buy from the venue box office directly or an accredited reseller.
- If you have already paid and suspect fraud:
  1. Contact your bank or payment provider immediately.
  2. File a report with the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
  3. File a report with the BBB Scam Tracker.
  4. Report the seller to the platform.

### Risk Signals Addressed

- Seller insists on WhatsApp, Telegram, or email for negotiation.
- Seller has a compelling personal story to justify urgency (emergency, family situation).
- Seller requests gift cards or wire transfer.
- Buyer is asked to pay before transfer is initiated.
