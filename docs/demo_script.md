# TicketGuard — Demo Script

Use the **killer demo** sample input for the main run. Use the **backup demo** if judges want to see a second example or if the first run has a glitch.

---

## 60-second version

**0:00–0:10**  
“TicketGuard helps fans spot risk in resale listings before they pay. I’ll paste a listing and get a risk assessment in one click.”

- [ ] Open the app (single page).
- [ ] Point to the large text area.

**0:10–0:22**  
“Here’s a listing that’s full of red flags: pay in 10 minutes, move to text, Venmo only.”

- [ ] Click **Load Example** (use the killer demo: “LAST 2 TICKETS… Venmo or Cash App in the next 10 minutes…”).
- [ ] Optionally set **Platform** = Facebook Marketplace, **Payment** = Venmo, **Off-platform** = Yes, **Urgency** = Yes.
- [ ] Click **Run Scam Triage**.

**0:22–0:45**  
“We get a risk level—here it’s High—confidence, and clear reasons with evidence from the text. There are safer next steps and copyable templates to message the seller or report to the platform.”

- [ ] Let results load (or show mock).
- [ ] Briefly show: risk badge, confidence, banner, top reason cards.
- [ ] Click one **Copy** on a template (verify message or platform report).
- [ ] Optionally expand **How we decided** (CoD log) for one line: “We use a chain-of-debate style so the model explains its reasoning.”

**0:45–0:60**  
“Everything stays on one page, no login. Built for the hackathon so judges can try it with their own text or our examples. That’s TicketGuard.”

- [ ] End on the results view or the input form with “Load Example” visible.

---

## 90-second version

**0:00–0:15**  
“TicketGuard helps fans quickly assess scam risk in resale ticket listings or seller chats. Judges paste a listing, click once, and get a risk level, reasons, and safer next steps—all without leaving the page.”

- [ ] Open the app.
- [ ] Show the input form: text area, platform, payment method, off-platform and urgency toggles, Run button, Load Example.

**0:15–0:30**  
“I’ll use a built-in example that’s designed to trigger high risk: strong urgency, off-platform payment, and Venmo.”

- [ ] Click **Load Example** and choose the killer demo.
- [ ] Confirm toggles match (off-platform and urgency on).
- [ ] Click **Run Scam Triage**.

**0:30–0:55**  
“The result shows High risk with calibrated confidence. Each reason ties back to the listing—for example, ‘Pay in 10 minutes’ and ‘Venmo only.’ We cite FTC, BBB, and Ticketmaster guidance. Down here are copyable templates: a verify message to the seller, a platform report, and containment steps if someone already paid.”

- [ ] Walk through: risk badge → confidence → banner → 2–3 reason cards (evidence_snippet + guidance_links).
- [ ] Show **Action steps**.
- [ ] Copy **verify_message** or **platform_report**.
- [ ] Expand **How we decided** and say: “We use a compact chain-of-debate so the model’s reasoning is visible—round A plans, round B red-team, round C revision, supervisor decision.”

**0:55–1:25**  
“We also have borderline and low-risk examples. For instance, if I load a listing that stays on-platform and uses PayPal with no time pressure, we’d expect lower risk and a ‘Proceed safer’ style message. The app is one page, no auth, mock-first so the demo always works even if the model isn’t wired yet. That’s TicketGuard—thanks.”

- [ ] Optionally click **Load Example** again and pick backup or low-risk, then Run (if time).
- [ ] End on a clear view of the UI.

---

## Exact order of clicks (60s)

1. Open app → land on page with form.
2. **Load Example** → killer demo populates.
3. (Optional) Set platform/payment/toggles.
4. **Run Scam Triage**.
5. Wait for results → show risk, reasons, templates.
6. Click **Copy** on one template.
7. (Optional) Expand **How we decided**.
8. End.

---

## Exact order of clicks (90s)

1. Open app.
2. Briefly show form and toggles.
3. **Load Example** → killer demo.
4. **Run Scam Triage**.
5. Walk through results: risk → reasons → action steps → templates → CoD.
6. Copy one template, expand CoD.
7. (Optional) Second **Load Example** (backup or low-risk) + **Run Scam Triage**.
8. End.

---

## What to say (key phrases)

- “One click” / “paste and click.”
- “Risk level, confidence, reasons with evidence from the text.”
- “Safer next steps and copyable templates.”
- “We cite FTC, BBB, Ticketmaster.”
- “Chain-of-debate so the model explains its reasoning.”
- “One page, no auth, mock-first for demo reliability.”
- “That’s TicketGuard.”

Keep it tight and judge-friendly.
